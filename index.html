LaFresCat: A Catalan Multi-Accent Speech Dataset for Text-to-Speech

To test LaFresCat, we chose Matcha-TTS, a state-of-the-art lightweight model trained with optimal-transport conditional flow matching, capable of generating refined mel spectrograms. A speaker embedding layer was added to learn different speaker attributes and, consequently, their accents. Since LaFresCat contains a total of 3.5 recorded hours, we set the 95% of the set for the training (2715), a 2% for validation (58) and the remaining 3% for testing (85). All utterances were mapped to phonemes with their corresponding accent-specific phonemizers. Additionally, to compensate for the size of LaFresCat, we applied transfer learning on previous Matcha-TTS checkpoints trained with English and Catalan out-of-the-box data. Analyzing Multi-Accented Inferences

Here we show aligned spectrograms with phonemes from the same inference generated with the four accents. The following spectrograms correspond to the sentence La seva gerra sembla molt antiga. This utterances shows three phonetic particularities that differentiate the Catalan variants. In the Balearic and Valencian accents, [t] is pronounced, whereas it is not pronounced in the other two accents. It can be seen that the -v- in seva is pronounced as [β] in Central accent while as [w] in Valencian. Also, it is observed that the /ʒ/ in gerra has an affricate pronunciation ([d͡ʒ]) in Valencian.
